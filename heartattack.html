Heart Attack First Aid

Heart Attack

  • Call for emergency medical help immediately.
  • Help the person relax by putting them in a seated position with their knees raised.
  • Ask about nitroglycerin: If the person carries nitroglycerin, ask them if they have taken it and help them take it if needed.
  • Administer aspirin: Give the person aspirin to chew or crush and swallow if they are not allergic to it. Aspirin helps to prevent blood clotting and can reduce heart damage during a heart attack.
  • Do not let the person lie down flat: Ask the person to recline on a sofa or sit upright.
  • Ask the person to cough: This can help force air into their lungs.
  • Monitor the person's symptoms: Symptoms of a heart attack can include chest pain, pressure, or tightness, pain or discomfort in the arm, back, neck, jaw, or stomach, shortness of breath, cold sweats, dizziness, lightheadedness, moderate-to-severe fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, and a sensation of severe indigestion.
